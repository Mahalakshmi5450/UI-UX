REPTILES

Reptiles are a class of cold-blooded vertebrates with dry, scaly skin that breathe air and usually lay eggs. They are cold-blooded, meaning they don't maintain a constant internal body temperature. They have lungs instead of gills, and their skin is made of scales, bony plates, or both.

Examples of reptiles include-

Geckos

Iguanas

Dinosaur

Skink

Turtles
