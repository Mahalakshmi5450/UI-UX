NeuroFlowers

NeuroFlowers is an interactive mobile game developed by the National Film Board of Canada (NFB). The game encourages players to nurture their minds, drawing a parallel between mental well-being and tending to a garden. Through a series of short games featuring colorful flowers and winged creatures, players sow seeds of change and reap the rewards of their efforts, promoting the idea that mental health requires regular care and attention.

The game was produced by the National Film Board of Canada, a public producer and distributor known for its creative documentaries, animations, and interactive works along with a few interns, namely- Laurence Thérien, Louis-Olivier Desmarais, Alexya Morin, Jakob Bouchard, Samuel Favreau and Maxim Bertrand who contributed to the project in the fields of Artistic Direction, Music And Sound Design, Research And Content, Programming and Production respectively; they also collaborated with Klô Pelgag for voice and Akufen.

The game was published in the year 2023 and its theme reflects a contemporary focus on mental health awareness and self-care. Culturally, there has been an increasing recognition of the importance of mental well-being, with numerous initiatives aimed at destigmatizing mental health issues and encouraging proactive care.

The NFB has a history of exploring innovative storytelling methods and addressing diverse topics through multimedia projects. The interactive nature of NeuroFlowers uses modern web technologies optimized for mobile devices. The rich audiovisual experience, featuring colorful graphics and immersive soundscapes, uses advanced animation techniques and frameworks that support interactive storytelling.
